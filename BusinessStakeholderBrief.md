# Business Stakeholder Brief — Olist E-Commerce Platform

---

## 🏢 Company Background

We are **Olist**, a Brazilian e-commerce marketplace that connects small and medium-sized sellers to major online retail channels under a single contract. We operate across multiple states in Brazil, handling thousands of orders monthly across diverse product categories — from electronics to fashion to home goods.

Over the past few years, we've scaled rapidly. We now have **thousands of active sellers**, a growing customer base spread across the country, and a logistics network that spans multiple carrier partners. Our revenue model depends on seller success — the more our sellers sell, the more we earn.

---

## ⚠️ Current Situation & Challenges

Despite our growth, the leadership team is facing mounting pressure. We are heading into our **annual strategic planning cycle**, and the board is asking hard questions we don't have clean answers to yet.

Specifically:

- We **don't fully understand where our revenue is coming from** — which sellers, which categories, which regions are actually driving the business vs. just adding operational noise
- We are **losing customers after their first purchase** and we don't know why or where exactly it's happening
- Our **operations team is getting complaints** about delivery performance, but we lack a clear picture of how widespread the problem is and whether it's regional or carrier-related
- We've been **spending marketing budget broadly** without knowing which customer segments or geographies deserve more investment
- Several of our **top sellers have quietly churned** in recent quarters, and retention of high-value sellers is becoming a boardroom concern

---

## 🎯 Business Problem Statement

> **We lack a consolidated, data-driven understanding of our marketplace performance across customers, sellers, orders, and payments — making it impossible to prioritize the right growth levers, fix operational bottlenecks, or retain our most valuable stakeholders (both buyers and sellers) at scale.**

---

## ✅ Business Goals — What Success Looks Like

| # | Goal | Success Metric |
|---|------|----------------|
| 1 | Understand revenue concentration and diversification | Know which top % of sellers/categories drive what % of revenue |
| 2 | Improve customer retention | Identify where and why customers are not returning |
| 3 | Fix delivery experience | Pinpoint states, routes, or periods with chronic delays |
| 4 | Smarter regional expansion | Know which geographies are underserved but high-potential |
| 5 | Protect our seller base | Identify at-risk or underperforming sellers before they churn |

---

## ❓ Key Business Questions for the Data Analyst

### 💰 Revenue & Sales Performance
1. Which product categories and sellers contribute the most to overall revenue — and how concentrated is that dependency?
2. Are there seasonal or monthly patterns in order volume and revenue that we should be planning around?
3. What is the average order value, and how does it vary across product categories?

### 👥 Customer Behaviour & Retention
4. What percentage of our customers are one-time buyers vs. repeat buyers?
5. Which states or cities have the highest customer concentration — and are we over-indexed in certain regions?
6. Is there a relationship between delivery experience and whether a customer comes back?

### 🚚 Operational & Delivery Performance
7. How often are orders delivered late vs. on time — and is this getting worse over time?
8. Which states or regions consistently experience the worst delivery delays?
9. Is there a gap between the estimated delivery date we promise customers and the actual delivery date?

### 🏪 Seller Performance
10. What does the distribution of sellers look like — are a small number of sellers carrying a disproportionate share of volume?
11. Are there sellers with high order volume but poor delivery or fulfilment metrics that could be damaging our brand?
12. Which seller locations (states) are underrepresented relative to where our customers are?

### 💳 Payments & Monetisation
13. What payment methods are customers preferring — and are there friction points we should address?
14. Are customers using instalment payments heavily, and what does that tell us about purchasing power across segments?
